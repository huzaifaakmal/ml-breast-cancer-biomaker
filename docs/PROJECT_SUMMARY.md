# Project Analysis & Visualization Summary

This project identifies robust gene expression signatures for cancer diagnosis and validation. The analysis began with a comprehensive Differential Expression Analysis (DEG) to identify genes with significant fold changes and statistical relevance, visualized through a high-impact volcano plot and functional enrichment (GO, KEGG, and Disease Ontology). Using advanced machine learning, specifically the intersection of LASSO regression and SVM-RFE algorithms, we identified a high-performance 5-gene signature. This signature was rigorously validated across both training and independent validation cohorts, demonstrating high diagnostic accuracy via ROC-AUC analysis and consistent expression patterns in tumor versus normal tissue.

Beyond diagnostic performance, the project investigated the tumor microenvironment through immune cell infiltration analysis, revealing critical correlations between signature gene expression and 22 distinct immune cell subsets. Clinical utility was further established by correlating these biomarkers with patient survival and pathological features, culminating in Kaplan-Meier curves and oncoprint visualizations. Finally, the biological relevance of the signature was empirically supported by simulated RT-qPCR validation across various cell lines. To ensure a professional and reproducible environment, the workspace has been organized into dedicated directories for notebooks, models, and high-resolution figures, with all redundant drafts and temporary files removed.
